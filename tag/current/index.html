Uniformity in Harmonic Analysis: Polynomial curves.

Inspired by previous works of, amongst others, Dendrinos, Stovall and Wright, I am working to show that certain operators in harmonic analysis parametrixed by polynomial families are uniformly bounded. This project aims to generalize previous known results over the Reals to Complex and p-Adic fields. Under the supervision of [Terence Tao](https://www.math.ucla.edu/~tao/). *(Links to the first ArXiv preprint in the project, more to come about Convolution, Decoupling, and p-Adic numbers.)*

Sparsity and training of overparametrised ReLu networks

This project aims to explain the convergence of over-parametrized ReLu networks with a single hidden layer. The key tool is exploiting sparsity properties of the minimizing solutions that follow from the homogeneity of the ReLu function. Joint project with [Joan Bruna](https://cims.nyu.edu/~bruna/). *(Links to an arXiv preprint)*

Decoupling properties of Cantor-type sets

The aim of this project is to prove decoupling estimates on both one-dimensional cantor sets and cantor sets composed with a moment curve. This work is inspired by the recent work of Stovall on Vinogradov estimates for ellipsephic sets, and the work of Laba and Wang on restriction for cantor sets. Joint project with [Alan Chang](https://sites.google.com/view/alanchang), [Rachel Greenfeld](https://www.math.ucla.edu/~greenfeld/), [Asgar Jamneshan](https://asgarjam.wixsite.com/home), [José Ramón Madrid Padilla](https://sites.google.com/site/joseramonmadridpadilla/home) and [Zane Li](https://zkli.pages.iu.edu/).

Monotonicity in decoupling

The goal of this project is to prove certain abstract decoupling inequalities and find proofs (or give counterexamples) to apparently elementary properties of decoupling in general settings. Joint project with [Tongou Yang](http://www.math.ubc.ca/~toyang/)
